# What makes Gai Jatra festival in Nepal special?
by [Paul Gurung](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/blog/author/Paul)
ON 24 Jul, 2025
![Himalayan Image](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/4852/biruwa.webp)
Gai Jatra is a traditional Hindu festival celebrated in Nepal, mainly in the Kathmandu Valley. In Nepal bhasa, this festival is referred to as 'saaparu.' It's a way for people to remember and honor their loved ones who passed away in the last year.
In Nepali, "Gai" means cow, and "Jatra" translates to festival or carnival in English. So, Gai Jatra is a lively event where families in Kathmandu dress up boys in amusing makeup and costumes, and they march through the streets in a grand procession of cows.
Although it's mainly celebrated by the Newar community, the festival holds significance for all citizens of Nepal. Even if people don't personally observe it, every Nepali is familiar with the festival, as it's highly respected throughout the country.
The Gai Jatra Festival is set to take place on August 20, 2024, which corresponds to the 4th day of the month of Bhadra in the Nepali calendar.
## Importance of Gai Jatra in Nepalese Culture
The Gai Jatra festival holds significant importance in Nepalese culture for various reasons.
  * **Remembering Loved Ones**


Gai Jatra provides a solemn occasion for families to remember and honor their departed loved ones. It serves as a way for people to pay tribute to those who have passed away in the preceding year.
  * **Cultural Legacy**


Rooted deeply in Nepalese cultural traditions, especially within the Newar community, the festival has been celebrated for centuries. It stands as a testament to the cultural identity of the Nepalese people, reflecting their rich heritage.
  * **Symbolic Journey**


The procession of cows carries spiritual symbolism, representing the journey of the departed souls. Cows held sacred in Hinduism, are believed to guide these souls to the afterlife.
  * **Tourist Attraction:**


Gai Jatra has garnered attention as a distinctive cultural event, attracting tourists interested in immersing themselves in and understanding the diverse tapestry of Nepalese traditions. This not only facilitates cultural exchange but also positively impacts the local economy through tourism.
![](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/4853/jatra2.webp)
## Story behind Gai Jatra in Nepal
In the 17th century, during King Pratap Malla's rule in Nepal, the origin of Gai Jatra, or the "Cow Festival," took place. The king, wanting to comfort his grieving queen after the loss of their son, initiated a festival where families mourning a recent loss could join a procession.
To add a touch of humor to the solemn event, the king decided to include cows in the procession, given their sacred status in Hinduism and belief in guiding souls to the afterlife. This period also marked the beginning of the tradition of dressing up young boys in various costumes, including those representing cows, gods, and mythical characters.
Over time, Gai Jatra has transformed into a cultural celebration that pays tribute to the departed, offers a platform for social commentary, and promotes community unity. The festival has grown to include participants from diverse ethnic backgrounds and communities, earning recognition as a distinctive cultural event in Nepal.
![](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/4854/community.webp)
## How do people celebrate Gai Jatra in Nepal?
Celebrating Gai Jatra in Nepal involves various traditional and cultural practices. Here's how people observe this festival:
### 1. Processions and Parades
Families who have experienced a loss in the past year take part in lively processions. These processions often feature a symbolic cow, representing the journey of the departed to the afterlife.
### 2. Dressing Up in Costumes
Young boys participate by donning diverse costumes, ranging from animals like cows to representations of gods and mythical figures. This tradition adds a touch of humor to the festival, offering a form of emotional release for grieving families.
### 3. Social and Political Commentary
Gai Jatra showcases satirical performances, skits, and humorous acts addressing current social and political issues. Participants use the festival as a platform to express opinions, raise awareness about societal matters, and encourage reflection.
### 4. Inclusive Participation
While originating in the Newar community, Gai Jatra has become more inclusive over time, with people from diverse ethnic backgrounds actively engaging in the festivities.
### 5. Offerings and Donations
Some families choose to make offerings and donations to the less fortunate as part of their observance of Gai Jatra.
### 6. Community Support
Gai Jatra provides a supportive environment for those coping with recent loss, allowing grieving families to share experiences, find solace, and receive emotional support.
**Conclusion**
In simple terms, the Gai Jatra festival in Nepal is a beautiful reflection of the country's diverse cultural fabric. It seamlessly weaves together the solemnity of remembrance with the joyous spirit of celebration.
The festival's distinctive blend of tradition, humor, and community bonds creates a truly unique and heartwarming experience. When families unite to honor their departed loved ones, and communities partake in lively processions, Gai Jatra emerges as a living manifestation of Nepal's cultural richness.
It not only nurtures unity and resilience but also embodies the enduring spirit of togetherness that defines the country's heritage.
**Frequently Asked Questions(FAQs)**
**Which god is Worshiped in Gai Jatra?**
The Cow Festival is widely celebrated in Nepal and holds significant popularity. The origins of the entire Gai Jatra festival trace back to ancient times when people revered and worshipped Yamaraj, the god of death.
**Who started Gai Jatra?**
The initiation of Gai Jatra dates back to the era of King Pratap Malla, who ruled from 1641 to 1671 AD. The festival was established in response to the untimely demise of his teenage son, Chakravartendra Malla, which deeply saddened the queen.
**Which caste celebrates Gai Jatra?**
Gai Jatra, or Sa Paru in the Nepal Bhasa language, is a festive tradition in Nepal, particularly embraced by the Newar community in the Kathmandu valley. This celebration serves as a tribute to the loved ones who have passed away in the preceding year.
**Why do people wear funny clothes and masks during Gai Jatra?**
During Gai Jatra, individuals don funny clothes and masks to bring humor to the festival, offering a way to cope with grief. This custom traces back to King Pratap Malla, who used humor to comfort his mourning queen after the loss of their son. The lighthearted costumes, featuring cows, gods, and mythical figures, play a crucial role in easing the emotional burden for families dealing with loss.
