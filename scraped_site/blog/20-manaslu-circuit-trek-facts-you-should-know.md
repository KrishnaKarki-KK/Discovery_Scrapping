# 20 Manaslu Circuit Trek Facts You Should Know
by [Paul Gurung](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/blog/author/Paul)
ON 24 Jul, 2025
![Himalayan Image](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5551/Manaslul-circuit-trek.webp)
The Manaslu Circuit Trek is an extraordinary adventure that takes you through some of Nepal's most remote and stunning landscapes. [Manaslu Circuit](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/trips/manaslu-circuit-trek) is famous for its remoteness, challenging terrain, and breathtaking views and offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural experiences, and historical significance. As you trek around the eighth-highest mountain in the world, Mount Manaslu, you'll encounter traditional villages, diverse flora and fauna, and awe-inspiring scenery that will leave you spellbound.
Here are 20 Manaslu Circuit Trek facts you should know.  
  

### **1. Remote and Less Crowded**
The Manaslu Circuit Trek offers a remote and less crowded alternative to other popular treks in Nepal, such as the Annapurna Circuit and Everest Base Camp. This trek allows you to experience the pristine beauty of the Himalayas without the hustle and bustle of crowded trails. The serenity and solitude of this trek make it an ideal choice for those seeking a peaceful trekking experience.
### **2. Minimum two-person requirement**
The Manaslu region is classified as a restricted area, meaning trekkers need a special permit to enter. This regulation helps preserve the natural environment and cultural heritage of the area. The permit process requires trekkers to be accompanied by a licensed guide and to trek in a group of at least two people.
### **3. Manaslu, the Eighth Highest Mountain**
At 8,163 meters (26,781 feet), Mount Manaslu is the eighth-highest mountain in the world. The trek takes you around this majestic peak, offering awe-inspiring views from various vantage points. The mountain's name, Manaslu, is derived from the Sanskrit word "Manasa," meaning "intellect" or "soul," which perfectly captures the spiritual essence of this region.
### **4. Challenging Terrain**
The Manaslu Circuit Trek is known for its challenging terrain, which includes steep ascents, rocky trails, and narrow paths along cliff edges. This trek requires a good level of physical fitness and endurance, making it a perfect adventure for seasoned trekkers looking for a demanding yet rewarding journey.
### **5. Rich Cultural Experience**
The trek passes through traditional Gurung and Tibetan villages, providing a rich cultural experience. You'll have the opportunity to interact with the locals, learn about their customs and traditions, and witness their way of life. The monasteries, prayer wheels, and Mani walls along the way add to the cultural richness of the trek.
### **6. Spectacular Scenery**
The Manaslu Circuit Trek offers some of the most spectacular scenery in Nepal, including lush forests, terraced fields, glacial rivers, and snow-capped peaks. The diverse landscapes and changing vistas keep the trek visually engaging and provide endless photo opportunities for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts.
### **7. Larkya La Pass**
One of the highlights of the Manaslu Circuit Trek is crossing the Larkya La Pass, which stands at 5,160 meters (16,930 feet). This high mountain pass offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding peaks, including Himlung Himal, Cheo Himal, and Kang Guru. The challenging ascent and descent of the pass make it a memorable part of the trek.
### **8. Tea House Trekking**
Unlike other remote treks that require camping, the Manaslu Circuit Trek offers tea house accommodations along the route. These tea houses provide basic lodging and meals, allowing trekkers to travel with lighter packs. The tea houses also present a unique opportunity to connect with fellow trekkers and share stories and experiences.
### **9. Diverse Flora and Fauna**
The Manaslu region is home to diverse flora and fauna, including several species of rhododendrons, orchids, and medicinal plants. The area is also a habitat for various wildlife, such as Himalayan tahr, snow leopards, and red pandas. Trekking through this region offers a chance to witness the rich biodiversity of the Himalayas.
### **10. UNESCO World Heritage Sites**
The Manaslu Circuit Trek starts from the town of Soti Khola and passes through several regions that are part of the Manaslu Conservation Area, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The conservation efforts in this area help protect the unique cultural and natural heritage, ensuring that future generations can enjoy its beauty.
![Manaslu region](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5552/23.webp)
### **11. Historical Significance**
The Manaslu region has historical significance, with ancient trade routes linking Nepal and Tibet. The trails you trek on were once used by traders transporting salt and other goods between the two regions. This historical connection adds an extra layer of intrigue to your trekking experience.
### **12. Breathtaking Suspension Bridges**
The trek features numerous suspension bridges spanning across deep gorges and glacial rivers. Walking across these swaying bridges offers a thrilling experience and breathtaking views of the valleys below. The bridges are a testament to the engineering ingenuity of the local people.
### **13. Altitude Sickness Awareness**
Altitude sickness is a real concern on the Manaslu Circuit Trek, especially when crossing the Larkya La Pass. You must know the symptoms and take precautions, such as acclimatization days, staying hydrated, and ascending gradually. Proper preparation and awareness can help prevent altitude-related issues. If you decide to trek with us, our expert guides will provide you with tips on how to avoid altitude sickness.
### **14. Guides and Porters**
Hiring a licensed guide and porter is mandatory for the Manaslu Circuit Trek. Guides provide valuable insights into the local culture, history, and geography, enhancing your trekking experience. Porters help carry your luggage, allowing you to focus on enjoying the trek and preserving your energy for the challenging sections.
### **15. Environmental Responsibility**
Trekkers are encouraged to follow eco-friendly practices, such as carrying reusable water bottles, avoiding plastic waste, and respecting the local environment. The principles of Leave No Trace are crucial in preserving the pristine beauty of the Manaslu region for future generations.
### **16. Variable Weather Conditions**
The weather in the Manaslu region can be unpredictable, with conditions ranging from hot and humid in the lower elevations to cold and windy in the higher altitudes. Trekkers should be prepared for variable weather by packing appropriate clothing and gear for all conditions.
### **17. Connectivity and Technology**
While the Manaslu Circuit Trek offers a remote and disconnected experience, some tea houses provide limited Wi-Fi and charging facilities. However, connectivity can be unreliable, so it’s best to embrace the opportunity to unplug and fully immerse yourself in the natural beauty and cultural richness of the trek.
### **18. Health and Safety**
Ensuring your health and safety is paramount on the Manaslu Circuit Trek. It is advisable to carry a well-stocked first aid kit and personal medications, and have travel insurance that covers high-altitude trekking. Please ensure your insurance covers helicopter evacuation up to the highest trek altitude as there are no adequate treatment centers in the region. Please call your insurer to double-check the helicopter evacuation. Staying informed about local health advisories and trekking conditions can also help prevent potential issues.
### **19. Cultural Sensitivity**
Respecting the local culture and traditions is essential when trekking in the Manaslu region. This includes dressing modestly, seeking permission before taking photographs of people, and being mindful of local customs and religious practices. Cultural sensitivity enhances your interactions with locals and enriches your trekking experience.
### **20. Post-Trek Exploration**
After completing the Manaslu Circuit Trek, consider exploring other attractions in Nepal. Kathmandu offers historical sites, vibrant markets, and cultural experiences, while other trekking routes like Annapurna and Everest regions provide additional adventure opportunities. 
So these are the 20 Manaslu Circuit Trek facts you should know before starting this trek.
From the challenging terrain and spectacular scenery to the rich cultural encounters and historical significance, every aspect of this trek is designed to push your limits and leave you with unforgettable memories.
As you complete your trek around the majestic Mount Manaslu, you'll not only have conquered a challenging route but also have gained a profound connection with Nepal’s natural and cultural heritage.
