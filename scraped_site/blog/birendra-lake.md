# Exploring Birendra Lake: A Trekker's Guide to Scenic Marvels
by [Paul Gurung](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/blog/author/Paul)
ON 24 Jul, 2025
![Himalayan Image](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5082/birend-lake.webp)
Birendra Lake is a pristine freshwater lake in the Gorkha district, near Samagaun Village in the Manaslu region. This tranquil lake offers not only breathtaking natural beauty but also an unforgettable trekking experience for those willing to embark on its trails. In this blog, we'll delve into the captivating allure of Birendra Lake, uncovering its trekking trails and scenic marvels for those eager to explore its wonders. It was formed from the Manaslu glaciers melting. 
## **Discovering Birendra Lake**
Birendra Lake, named after the late King Birendra of Nepal, is a shimmering alpine lake in the Manaslu Conservation Area near the Punhyen Glacier. This lake sits surrounded by snow-capped peaks, lush forests, and meandering streams while exuding a sense of serenity and tranquility. On a clear day, you can see the surrounding peaks reflect on its waters.  
  

## **Trekking Trails**
During your Manaslu Circuit Trek, as you embark on your journey through the breathtaking landscapes of the Manaslu region from Samagaun, you can take a brief detour to a tranquil escape.
The distance from Samagaun to Birendra Lake (Tal) is about 1.88 miles long, with an elevation gain of 394 ft, and takes about 1–2.5 hours to complete. The trail is considered moderately challenging and is great for camping and hiking. There are guest houses, lodges, and other sites for overnight stays in Samagaun. Nearby, there are also base camps to stay at if you do not want to hike back to Samaguan. 
As you approach the lake, you'll be greeted by the sight of crystal-clear waters reflecting the surrounding snow-capped peaks, creating a picture-perfect scene.
We request you to take a moment to pause and soak in the serene ambiance, surrounded by the tranquility of nature. You can sit by the tranquil shores, enjoying a peaceful moment of reflection amidst the majestic Himalayan landscape. 
Whether you spend just a few minutes or linger a bit longer, a visit to Birendra Lake adds a memorable touch to your Manaslu Circuit Trek.   
  

## **Scenic Marvels**
The beauty of Birendra Lake extends far beyond its pristine waters. You can explore the countless scenic marvels in its vicinity. Trekkers can venture along the lake's shoreline, basking in the tranquility of the surroundings and soaking in the natural splendor. For the more adventurous, nearby hiking trails offer opportunities to ascend to higher vantage points, providing sweeping views of the lake and its surroundings. As the sun sets behind the mountains, casting a golden glow upon the landscape, you can enjoy a spectacle of color and light that is truly awe-inspiring.
## **When to view Birendra Lake?**
The trekking season for Birendra Lake within the Manaslu Circuit coincides with two main windows of favorable weather: from late September to early November (fall season) and from March to May (spring season). These months are considered the best times to trek in the Himalayas due to stable weather conditions, clear skies, and moderate temperatures, making for comfortable and enjoyable hiking experiences.
During the fall season, the weather is typically dry and stable, with clear skies offering stunning views of the neighboring mountains. The temperatures are mild during the day, making it pleasant for trekking, while the nights can be cooler at higher altitudes. The autumn months also coincide with the post-monsoon period, resulting in lush greenery and blooming rhododendron forests, adding to the scenic beauty of the trek.
In spring, the weather begins to warm up after the winter months, and the landscape comes alive with colorful flowers and blossoming vegetation. The skies remain clear, providing excellent visibility of the snow-capped peaks, including the towering Himalayan giants surrounding Birendra Lake. The spring months are also known for being relatively less crowded compared to the fall season, allowing trekkers to enjoy a more peaceful and serene trekking experience.
However, you must prepare for potential weather fluctuations, especially at higher altitudes, where conditions can change rapidly. You should also be aware of the possibility of occasional rain or snow showers, particularly in the shoulder seasons. 
Ultimately, regardless of the season chosen for trekking to Birendra Lake, careful planning, proper acclimatization, and a flexible mindset are essential for a safe and enjoyable adventure in the spectacular landscapes of the Manaslu region.

## **Things to keep in mind**
  * Ensure you have the necessary permits for trekking in the Annapurna Conservation Area.
  * Dress in layers and be prepared for changing weather conditions, especially at higher elevations.
  * Stay hydrated and take frequent breaks to acclimatize to the altitude.
  * Respect the local culture and customs, and always ask for permission before taking photographs of people or their property.
  * Leave no trace and practice responsible tourism. Dispose of waste properly and minimize your impact on the environment.  
  



## **Conclusion**
Birendra Lake stands as a testament to the natural beauty and splendor of the Nepalese Himalayas. With its pristine waters, stunning vistas, and unparalleled trekking opportunities, it beckons adventurers and nature lovers worldwide. Whether you're seeking a challenging trekking adventure or simply wish to immerse yourself in the tranquility of nature, Birendra Lake offers an experience like no other. If you want to view the famous Birendra Lake, we invite you for a [Manaslu Circuit Trek](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/trips/manaslu-circuit-trek).
