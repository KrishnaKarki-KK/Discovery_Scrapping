# 20 Best Viewpoints in the Everest Region
by [Paul Gurung](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/blog/author/Paul)
ON 24 Jul, 2025
![Himalayan Image](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5210/acircuit.webp)
The Everest region, located in northeastern Nepal, offers some of the most breathtaking views of the Himalayas, including the majestic Mount Everest. Here are 20 of the best viewpoints in this region:  
  

## **1. Kala Patthar  
**
Kala Patthar is one of the best viewpoints in the Everest region, offering one of the most iconic and breathtaking panoramas.
At 5,545 meters (18,192 feet), Kala Patthar is a trekkers' paradise. As you ascend this rocky ridge, each step brings you closer to awe-inspiring vistas. The climb is challenging, but the reward is a 360-degree view of the Himalayas, with Mount Everest, Nuptse, and Lhotse dominating the skyline.
For most trekkers, the highlight of Kala Patthar is witnessing the sunrise or sunset. Imagine the first rays of dawn touching the peak of Everest or the golden hues of the setting sun casting long shadows over the snow-capped mountains. These moments are magical and provide perfect opportunities for photography, leaving you with memories that last a lifetime.
While Everest Base Camp offers a close-up view of the Khumbu Icefall, Kala Patthar provides a much more comprehensive perspective. From this vantage point, you can see the South Face of Everest and the impressive West Ridge, making it an ideal spot for those who want to experience the grandeur of the world's highest mountain without the extreme altitudes of a summit attempt.  
![](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5211/kp.webp)  

## **2. Gokyo Ri**
At an impressive altitude of 5,357 meters (17,575 feet), Gokyo Ri is renowned for its stunning views. The ascent may be challenging, but the rewards are immense. As you reach the summit, a sweeping vista unfolds before you, featuring some of the highest peaks on Earth, including Mount Everest, Lhotse, Makalu, and Cho Oyu.
The journey to Gokyo Ri takes you through the Gokyo Valley, a land of natural wonders. Along the way, you'll pass the mesmerizing Gokyo Lakes, a series of six glacial-fed lakes known for their striking blue-green hues. 
Much like Kala Patthar, Gokyo Ri is an exceptional place to witness sunrise and sunset. At dawn, the peaks light up with the first golden rays, creating a magical scene that feels almost otherworldly. In the evening, the sun casts a warm glow over the landscape, offering perfect moments for photography and reflection.
From the summit of Gokyo Ri, you'll enjoy a panoramic view. The Everest range, including the elusive northwest face of Mount Everest, appears in full glory. The sight of the immense Ngozumpa Glacier, the longest in the Himalayas, adds to the breathtaking scenery. This view provides a different perspective on Everest, showcasing the mountain's grandeur from a unique angle.  
![](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5212/gri.webp)  

## **3. Tengboche Monastery**
In addition to being a spiritual center, Tengboche (3,867 meters) offers panoramic views of Everest, Ama Dablam, Nuptse, and Thamserku. The monastery is an exceptional blend of cultural richness and natural splendor, making it a must-visit for trekkers and nature enthusiasts.
Tengboche Monastery, built in 1916, is the spiritual heart of the Khumbu region. It is the largest gompa in the area and serves as a key religious center for the Sherpa community. The monastery's intricate architecture, vibrant murals, and serene atmosphere provide a deep sense of tranquility, attracting not only trekkers but also those seeking spiritual solace.
From this monastery, you will get panoramic views of some of the earth's highest and most magnificent peaks - Mount Everest, Lhotse, Ama Dablam, and other towering giants. The clear, unobstructed views make Tengboche one of the best spots to appreciate the grandeur of the Himalayas.  
![](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5213/kkllp.webp)  

## **4. Nangkartshang Peak/ Nangkar Tshang Peak**
Nangkar Tshang Peak, standing at approximately 5,083 meters (16,676 feet), offers one of the most spectacular panoramas of Mount Everest. This peak lies near the village of Dingboche, and it is a perfect destination for those seeking a less crowded vantage point to marvel at the world’s tallest peak. The ascent to Nangkar Tshang is challenging, but rewarding as trekkers traverse steep trails adorned with prayer flags, rugged terrain, and pristine alpine scenery.
The journey to Nangkar Tshang Peak not only provides breathtaking views of Everest but also offers stunning vistas of other majestic peaks, including Lhotse, Makalu, and Ama Dablam. The climb, typically part of the acclimatization process for those on the Everest Base Camp trek, allows trekkers to gradually adjust to the high altitude while enjoying the serene beauty of the Khumbu region. At the summit, the sight of Everest towering over the horizon, surrounded by a sea of snow-capped mountains, is a sight that leaves a lasting impression on all who witness it.  
  

## **5. Renjo La Pass**
Renjo La Pass, at an impressive 5,360 meters, is one of the most remarkable viewpoints for trekkers seeking unparalleled vistas of Mount Everest. This high-altitude pass provides breathtaking panoramic views of Everest, Lhotse, and the stunning turquoise waters of the Gokyo Lakes below. The journey to Renjo La Pass is a challenging trek, often part of the Gokyo Lakes circuit, that rewards adventurers with a serene and less crowded experience compared to other trekking routes in the region. The ascent involves navigating steep, rocky terrain and is known for its stunning scenery and sense of solitude amidst the towering peaks.
From the top of Renjo La Pass, you can see a vast expanse of the Himalayas, offering a unique perspective of Everest. The tranquil Gokyo Lakes, reflecting the sky and mountains, add to the surreal beauty of the landscape. This vantage point is particularly spectacular at sunrise or sunset when the golden light casts an ethereal glow over the snow-capped peaks and deep blue lakes.   
  

## **6. Cho La Pass**
At an elevation of 5,420 meters, Cho La Pass is another awe-inspiring viewpoint in the Everest region, known for dramatic vistas of towering peaks and expansive glaciers. This pass forms a crucial link between the Gokyo Valley and the Everest Base Camp trek, making it a popular yet challenging route for seasoned trekkers. The ascent to Cho La Pass involves crossing a glacier, navigating icy paths, and climbing steep rocky sections.
From the top of Cho La Pass, you can view stunning views of the surrounding Himalayan giants, including Everest, Ama Dablam, Cholatse, and Baruntse. The sight of the massive glaciers and the rugged terrain is humbling and exhilarating. The pristine, icy landscape under the bright blue sky creates a stark and beautiful contrast, offering a true sense of the raw power and majesty of the Himalayas.   
  

## **7. Kongma La Pass**
Kongma La Pass, standing at 5,545 meters, offers some of the most fantastic views in the Everest region, including the formidable Khumbu Glacier, the towering Makalu, and the entire Everest range. This pass is part of the Three Passes Trek, renowned for its demanding trails and breathtaking scenery. The ascent to Kongma La is challenging and requires you to navigate steep ascents, rocky paths, and potentially snow-covered trails, making it suitable for experienced adventurers seeking a challenging and rewarding experience.
Upon reaching the summit of Kongma La Pass, you will be rewarded with sweeping vistas of some of the highest peaks in the world. The view of the Khumbu Glacier below, with its intricate patterns of ice and crevasses, is particularly striking. On clear days, the panorama includes the majestic Everest, Lhotse, and the distant Makalu, creating a truly awe-inspiring backdrop. The sense of isolation and the pristine natural beauty of this high-altitude pass provides a unique and unforgettable trekking experience in the heart of the Himalayas.  
![](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5214/3-passes.webp)  

## **8. Ama Dablam Base Camp**
Ama Dablam Base Camp, at 4,600 meters, offers trekkers a close-up view of one of the most stunning and iconic peaks in the Himalayas, Ama Dablam. However, it also provides equally stunning views of Mount Everest.
This mountain is often referred to as the "Matterhorn (a mountain in the Alps, Europe) of the Himalayas," and is renowned for its striking, pyramid-like shape and sheer beauty. The trek to the base camp is relatively accessible compared to higher altitude passes, making it a popular excursion for those looking to experience the grandeur of the Himalayas without the extreme altitude challenges.
Ama Dablam rises sharply against the sky, its steep faces and ridges creating a dramatic and awe-inspiring sight. Surrounding the base camp are other impressive peaks and the lush valleys of the Khumbu region, offering a rich tapestry of natural beauty. The base camp is also a place to reflect on the mountaineering feats and the spirit of adventure that Ama Dablam embodies.  
  

## **9. Chhukung Ri**
Chhukung Ri, rising to 5,550 meters, is a prominent viewpoint that offers panoramic views of some of the Himalayas' most famous peaks, including Everest, Lhotse, Ama Dablam, and Makalu. This peak is a popular acclimatization hike for those preparing for higher climbs or treks in the region. The ascent to Chhukung Ri is steep and demanding, but the rewards are immense, with uninterrupted vistas of towering summits and the vast expanses of the surrounding valleys.
At the summit of Chhukung Ri, the 360-degree views are breathtaking. The proximity to Ama Dablam provides an incredibly detailed and close perspective of its majestic slopes. The landscape is a mosaic of rugged mountain terrain, glaciers, and deep valleys, all illuminated by the clear Himalayan light. 
## **  
10. Everest Base Camp**
Everest Base Camp, at 5,364 meters, is an iconic destination for trekkers and mountaineers alike, offering unparalleled views of the Khumbu Icefall and the South Face of Everest. This base camp serves as the starting point for climbers attempting to reach the summit of Everest, and the journey to this spot is a pilgrimage for adventure enthusiasts worldwide. The trek to Everest Base Camp takes you through diverse landscapes, including lush forests, Sherpa villages, and arid high-altitude deserts, culminating in the stark beauty of the base camp itself.
From Everest Base Camp, you will also enjoy the views of Khumbu Icefall, which is both awe-inspiring and humbling. The icefall, with its towering seracs and deep crevasses, presents a formidable challenge for climbers and a dramatic spectacle for trekkers. The South Face of Everest looms above, offering you a close-up perspective of the world's highest peak.   
![](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5215/gghhf.webp)  

## **11. Pangboche**
Pangboche, at 3,985 meters, is a picturesque village that offers a great view of Everest and the surrounding peaks. It is one of the oldest Sherpa villages in the Khumbu region and is home to the famous Pangboche Monastery. The village is a frequent stop for trekkers on their way to Everest Base Camp, providing a cultural and spiritual dimension to the trekking experience. The views from Pangboche encompass Everest, Ama Dablam, and Thamserku, creating a stunning backdrop to this serene village.
The vantage point at Pangboche allows you to appreciate the scale and beauty of Everest and its neighboring peaks. The traditional Sherpa culture, combined with the natural beauty, makes Pangboche a memorable stop on the trek. This village's location offers a unique perspective of the mountains, framed by the rugged terrain and the clear Himalayan skies. Pangboche provides a perfect blend of cultural richness and natural splendor, enhancing the overall trekking experience in the Everest region.  
  

## **12. Phortse**
Phortse village, located at 3,840 meters, provides stunning views of Everest, Ama Dablam, Thamserku, and Khumbila, creating a panoramic spectacle that is both beautiful and serene. The village is also known for its traditional Sherpa houses and terraced fields, adding to its picturesque charm.
The perspective from Phortse allows trekkers to enjoy the dramatic beauty of Ama Dablam up close, along with a sweeping vista of other Himalayan giants. The tranquility of the village, combined with the stunning natural scenery, makes Phortse a hidden gem in the Everest region.  
  

## **13. Dingboche**
Dingboche, at 4,410 meters, is a popular stop for trekkers in the Everest region, offering magnificent views of Everest, Ama Dablam, Lhotse, and Island Peak. This village serves as an acclimatization stop for those heading to higher altitudes, providing both comfort and stunning scenery. The wide valley of Dingboche offers expansive views and a sense of openness, with the towering peaks of the Himalayas as a breathtaking backdrop.
The village's location and altitude make it an ideal spot for acclimatization, allowing trekkers to soak in the beauty of the surrounding mountains while preparing for the next stages of their journey.  
  

## **14. Namche Bazaar**
Namche Bazaar, situated at 3,440 meters, is a bustling hub in the Everest region and a popular acclimatization spot for trekkers. Namche Bazaar, often called the gateway to Everest, offers beautiful views of Kongde Ri, Thamserku, and Everest, itself. This vibrant Sherpa town is a natural amphitheater, providing a stunning backdrop of the surrounding peaks and a lively atmosphere with its shops, cafes, and markets.
From Namche Bazaar, trekkers can enjoy panoramic views of some of the most iconic mountains in the Himalayas. The sight of Everest peeking over the ridges is a thrilling preview of what lies ahead, while the closer views of Kongde Ri and Thamserku add to the scenic splendor. This town's strategic location and altitude make it an essential stop for acclimatization, allowing you to enjoy the natural beauty and Sherpa culture that Namche Bazaar offers.  
![](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5216/gggkk.webp)  

## **15. Khunde**
Khunde, at 3,840 meters, is a picturesque village that also offers excellent views of Everest, Thamserku, and Ama Dablam. This traditional Sherpa village in the Khumbu region provides a quieter alternative to the more frequented trekking routes. The views from Khunde also include the dramatic slopes of Thamserku and the iconic Ama Dablam, creating a stunning panorama that showcases the natural beauty of the Himalayas.
The vantage point from Khunde allows trekkers to immerse themselves in the serene and majestic landscape of the Everest region. The village, with its traditional stone houses and terraced fields, adds to the picturesque charm. For those seeking a peaceful retreat with breathtaking mountain views, Khunde offers a perfect blend of natural splendor and cultural richness, enhancing the overall trekking experience.  
  

## **16. Khumjung**
Khumjung, located at 3,790 meters, is a beautiful village providing picturesque views of several mountains, including Everest. This village is known for its traditional Sherpa culture and the historic Khumjung Monastery.
The views from Khumjung allow you to enjoy a serene and beautiful setting, with the towering peaks providing a majestic backdrop. The village's location and altitude make it an ideal spot for acclimatization, offering both comfort and spectacular views. The combination of cultural heritage and natural beauty makes Khumjung a must-visit destination for those trekking in the Everest region.  
  

## **17. Lobuche**
Lobuche, at 4,940 meters, is a crucial stop for trekkers heading to Everest Base Camp, offering views of the Lobuche peaks, the Khumbu Glacier, and Everest. This high-altitude village provides a challenging yet rewarding experience for trekkers, with its proximity to some of the most iconic mountains in the Himalayas. The views from Lobuche include the rugged terrain of the Lobuche peaks and the vast expanse of the Khumbu Glacier, creating a dramatic and awe-inspiring landscape.
From Lobuche, the sight of the surrounding peaks and the glacier below is humbling and exhilarating at the same time. The village's altitude and location make it an essential stop for acclimatization, allowing trekkers to prepare for the final push to Everest Base Camp. The stunning natural scenery and the sense of adventure that permeates Lobuche make it a memorable and vital part of the trekking journey in the Everest region.  
  

## **18. Dughla**
Dughla offers trekkers a unique vantage point. Located at 4,620 meters, it provides stunning views of surrounding peaks like Cholatse and Taboche. The landscape around Dugla contains memorials of climbers who have lost their lives on Everest, adding a somber, yet inspirational element to the breathtaking scenery.
As you ascend from Dughla, you will get panoramic views of the Khumbu Glacier and the towering peaks around. This spot serves as a poignant reminder of the challenges and triumphs of high-altitude trekking, making it a meaningful stop on your journey to Everest Base Camp.  
  

## **19. Gorak Shep**
Gorak Shep, at 5,164 meters, is a crucial waypoint for trekkers heading to Everest Base Camp, offering a good vantage point for views of Everest and the surrounding peaks. This remote settlement serves as the final acclimatization stop before reaching the base camp, providing both shelter and stunning scenery. The views from Gorak Shep include Everest, Nuptse, and Pumori, creating a breathtaking panorama that marks the culmination of the trekking journey.
The village's high altitude and strategic location make it an essential stop for Everest Base Camp trekkers, allowing them to acclimate and prepare for the final leg of the journey. The dramatic views and the sense of anticipation that permeates Gorak Shep make it a highlight of the trek, offering a front-row seat to the majesty of the Himalayas.  
  

## **20. Thame**
Thame, at 3,800 meters, is a picturesque village that offers views of Everest, Thamserku, and the Rolwaling range. This traditional Sherpa village is less frequented by trekkers, providing a more peaceful and intimate experience of the Khumbu region. The views from Thame include the distant peaks of Everest and the closer, dramatic slopes of Thamserku and the Rolwaling range, creating a stunning and serene landscape.
The vantage point from Thame is one of the best viewpoints in the Everest region that   
allows trekkers to immerse themselves in nature's grandeur and tranquility of the Himalayas. The village's traditional culture and serene environment add to its charm, making it a perfect retreat for those seeking a quieter trekking experience. The stunning views and cultural richness make Thame a hidden gem in the Everest region, offering a unique and memorable perspective of the world's highest mountains.
## **Finally,**
These are the best viewpoints in the Everest region that offer a mix of high-altitude challenges and more accessible locations, ensuring that trekkers of all levels can experience the awe-inspiring beauty of the [Everest region](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/trips/everest-base-camp-trek).
