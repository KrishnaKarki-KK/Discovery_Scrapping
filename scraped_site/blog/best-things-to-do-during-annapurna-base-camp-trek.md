# 25 Best things to do during Annapurna Base Camp Trek
by [Paul Gurung](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/blog/author/Paul)
ON 24 Jul, 2025
![Himalayan Image](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5486/featured.webp)
The Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) Trek is one of Nepal's most iconic trekking routes, offering diverse experiences from stunning mountain vistas to rich cultural encounters. In this article, we present you the 25 best things to do during Annapurna Base Camp Trek.
### 1. Explore Pokhara
Pokhara is the tourist capital of Nepal, and you will pass through Pokhara during the ABC trek. We recommend you spend some time in Pokhara. As you wander through this vibrant city, you'll be captivated by the stunning views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges, which create a picturesque backdrop to your adventures. Start your day with a serene boat ride on Phewa Lake, where the reflection of the mountains on the calm water creates a breathtaking sight. Don't miss the opportunity to visit the Tal Barahi Temple, situated on an island in the lake, which adds a touch of spiritual tranquility to your journey.
As you explore Pokhara, you'll find many activities to fuel your adventurous spirit. Explore the mystical Mahendra and Bat Caves, or hike up to the World Peace Pagoda for panoramic views of the valley below. The bustling Lakeside area offers vibrant nightlife, delicious local cuisine, and a variety of shops where you can find unique souvenirs. Whether paragliding over the city, discovering hidden waterfalls, or simply soaking in the natural beauty, Pokhara promises an unforgettable experience for every traveler.
### 2. Visit the Gurung Museum in Ghandruk
Ghandruk, a beautiful village on the trekking route, is home to the Gurung Museum. The Gurung Museum will introduce you to Gurung culture, traditional dress, and unique lifestyle. The museum showcases artifacts, traditional clothing, tools, and photographs that chronicle the Gurung people's way of life.
In the Gurung Museum, you can explore exhibits highlighting various aspects of Gurung culture, including their agricultural practices, religious rituals, and unique architecture. The museum's collection often includes traditional dresses adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, reflecting the community's craftsmanship and artistic traditions.
The museum's location in Ghandruk, surrounded by terraced fields and stunning mountain views, enhances the cultural immersion experience, making it a must-visit destination for trekkers and cultural enthusiasts alike.
![](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/5488/description.webp)
### 3. Experience a Traditional Gurung Dance
When trekking through villages like Ghandruk and Chhomrong, you can experience a traditional Gurung dance, a highlight of the cultural immersion along the Annapurna Base Camp trek. The local communities organize these treks to entertain trekkers and share their rich cultural heritage. The dances are vibrant and captivating, with dancers dressed in colorful traditional attire, moving gracefully to the rhythm of folk music played on traditional instruments.
As you watch, you'll see intricate choreography that reflects the Gurung people's history, traditions, and everyday life. Participating in or observing these dances creates a connection with the local culture, making your trekking adventure memorable and authentic.
### 4. Soak in Jhinu Danda Hot Springs
After a long day of trekking through the Annapurna region, there's nothing quite like soaking in the natural hot springs of Jhinu Danda to rejuvenate your tired muscles. The hot springs lie on the banks of the Modi Khola River. As you submerge yourself in the warm, mineral-rich waters, you'll feel the tension and fatigue of the day's hike melt away. The setting is serene, surrounded by lush greenery and the soothing sounds of the river, creating a perfect ambiance for relaxation.
### 5. Capture the Sunrise from Poon Hill
Annapurna Base Camp trek includes a visit to Poon Hill, make sure you don't miss the sunrise from Poon Hill. Rising early and hiking up to the viewpoint from Ghorepani in the pre-dawn darkness is well worth the effort.
As the first rays of sunlight break, you'll witness the panoramic views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges gradually being bathed in golden light. The sight of these majestic mountains glowing in the early morning light is breathtaking and offers a perfect opportunity for photography. The serene and awe-inspiring atmosphere atop Poon Hill during sunrise is a moment of pure magic, leaving a lasting impression and providing one of the most memorable highlights of your trek.
### 6. Enjoy the Rhododendron Forests
In spring, the rhododendron forests along the Annapurna Base Camp trek burst into a spectacular display of colors, creating a vibrant canopy of red, pink, and white blossoms. As you walk through these enchanting forests, you'll enter a magical world. The vibrant hues of the flowers contrast beautifully with the lush green foliage, adding a delightful splash of color to your journey. The sweet fragrance of the blooming rhododendrons fills the air, enhancing the sensory experience. This natural beauty will also uplift your spirits.
### 7. Visit the Annapurna Conservation Area Project (ACAP) Office
The Annapurna Conservation Area Project (ACAP) office in Ghandruk offers a unique chance to learn about the region's conservation efforts. You'll discover information on the diverse flora and fauna, the challenges of preserving this biodiversity, and the initiatives to protect the environment. This visit helps you appreciate the natural beauty of the Annapurna region and understand the importance of maintaining it for future generations.
At the ACAP office, you'll also learn about sustainable trekking practices. They provide guidelines on responsible waste management, trail etiquette, and ways to minimize your ecological footprint. You'll gain insights into supporting local communities through eco-friendly tourism. This knowledge helps you to preserve the Annapurna region, ensuring its pristine landscapes remain unspoiled for future adventurers.
### 8. Admire the Machapuchare (Fishtail) Mountain
The Machapuchare (Fishtail) Mountain, is famous for its unique fishtail shape and revered as a sacred peak that's never been climbed. Standing at 6,993 meters, this majestic mountain mesmerizes trekkers with its stunning beauty and spiritual aura. The view of Machapuchare from various points along the trek is genuinely awe-inspiring, evoking a deep appreciation for the natural world.
As you journey through the Annapurna region, you'll encounter Machapuchare frequently, each sighting more breathtaking than the last. Whether glowing in the golden light of sunrise or nestled among the lush, green landscapes of the monsoon season, Machapuchare's presence adds a touch of magic to your trek.
### 9. Taste Traditional Nepali Cuisine
During the trek, you can savor the rich flavors of traditional Nepali cuisine. From hearty dal bhat (lentil soup with rice) to momos (dumplings) filled with savory ingredients, the local food offers a delicious and authentic taste of Nepal's culinary heritage.
Throughout your journey, you can try several dishes prepared with fresh, locally sourced ingredients. Enjoying local meals in teahouses and lodges along the trail not only refuels your energy but also connects you with the local culture and traditions, making your trekking experience even more memorable.
### 10. Stay in a Teahouse During the ABC Trek
ABC trek also provides you a chance to experience the warmth and hospitality of the local culture by staying in teahouses during the Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) trek. These cozy lodges provide a welcoming place to rest, offering basic amenities like comfortable beds, hot meals, and the chance to connect with fellow trekkers.
In the tea houses, you can enjoy traditional Nepali hospitality, share stories with other adventurers, and savor the unique atmosphere of these mountain retreats, making your journey through the Annapurna region even more enriching and enjoyable.
### 11. Cross the Modi Khola River
Crossing the Modi Khola River can be a delightful experience during the Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) trek. This river, fed by glacial meltwaters from the Annapurna range, winds through the valleys, providing a refreshing and scenic backdrop to your journey.
The experience of crossing the Modi Khola varies from suspension bridges swaying high above the rushing waters to serene, shallow fords. Each crossing offers adventure and a chance to marvel at the raw, natural beauty of the region, enhancing the sense of exploration and connection to the landscape that defines the ABC trek.
### 12. Visit Bamboo and Dovan
Visiting Bamboo and Dovan on the Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) trek offers a serene and picturesque experience. Bamboo, nestled amidst dense bamboo forests, provides a tranquil atmosphere where you can enjoy the lush greenery and the soothing sounds of nature. It's an ideal spot to rest and take in the serene surroundings after a day of trekking.
Continuing to Dovan, you are greeted by the enchanting beauty of rhododendron forests and cascading waterfalls. This peaceful village offers a quiet retreat with stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Both Bamboo and Dovan serve as perfect stops to relax, recharge, and immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the Annapurna region, making your trek even more memorable.
### 13. Climb the Stone Steps to Chhomrong
Climbing the stone steps to Chhomrong is a challenging yet rewarding part of the Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) trek. These steps wind up through terraced fields and charming villages, offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.
As you ascend, the quaint village of Chhomrong gradually comes into view. The climb, though strenuous, is punctuated by the welcoming smiles of local villagers and the refreshing mountain air, making the effort worthwhile.
### 14. Explore Caves in Deurali
Exploring Deurali's caves is an adventurous highlight of the Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) trek. These intriguing rock formations offer a fascinating glimpse into the geological history and natural beauty of this region.
As you venture into the caves, you'll be surrounded by the cool, damp air and the echo of your footsteps, creating an atmosphere of mystery and exploration. The caves provide a unique opportunity to take a break from trekking, delve into nature's hidden wonders, and add an element of adventure to your journey through the Annapurna region.
### 15. Spot Wildlife
Spotting wildlife along the Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) trek adds an exciting and enriching dimension to your adventure. The Annapurna region is home to several animals, including langur monkeys, Himalayan thar, and various colorful bird species such as pheasants and eagles.
As you trek through forests, meadows, and alpine landscapes, look attentively to these fascinating creatures in their natural habitats. Observing wildlife enhances your connection to the environment and offers unforgettable moments of wonder and discovery amidst the stunning scenery of the Annapurna region.
### 16. Savor the Annapurna Base Camp
At the Annapurna Base Camp, you must take a moment to savor your extraordinary achievement. The crisp mountain air, the serene ambiance, and the stunning 360-degree views create a perfect backdrop for reflection and appreciation. We hope you will engage with fellow trekkers and locals to share stories and immerse yourself in the vibrant culture that thrives even at this high altitude. Every direction you look, there's a new aspect of nature's beauty to admire, making your time at Annapurna Base Camp a truly unforgettable highlight of your journey.
### 17. Visit the Hinku Cave Rock
As you trek towards the Annapurna Base Camp, you'll encounter the intriguing Hinku Cave Rock. This massive overhanging rock provides a unique respite while trekking.
Hinku Cave Rock isn't just a practical stop but a spot imbued with a sense of history and mystique. As you take a break here, you can marvel at the rugged beauty surrounding you and feel a deeper connection to the countless trekkers who have sought refuge under this ancient rock over the years. Moments like these make the Annapurna Base Camp trek truly unforgettable.
### 18. Interact with Local Children
Engaging with local children during your Himalayan trek enriches your experience with genuine cultural exchanges. Their curiosity and friendliness provide a heartwarming glimpse into daily life in the mountain villages, creating moments of joy and connection. Sharing stories, learning local games, or simply enjoying a smile together fosters a deeper appreciation for the vibrant cultures that thrive in these remote areas.
### 19. Photograph the Annapurna Sanctuary
During the trek, you can capture the Annapurna Sanctuary through your lens, a photographer's dream. The sanctuary, a high glacial basin surrounded by a ring of majestic peaks, offers a stunning backdrop for your photos. The interplay of light and shadow on the snow-capped mountains, the vibrant colors of the alpine meadows, and the serene beauty of the landscape create endless opportunities for breathtaking shots. Every angle reveals a new facet of this natural wonder, making it a rewarding experience for any photography enthusiast.
### 20. Learn About the Flora
The Annapurna region is home to an incredible variety of plant life, offering a rich tapestry of flora to explore. From the lush rhododendron forests that burst into vibrant bloom during the spring to the hardy alpine plants that thrive in harsh high-altitude conditions, each step of your trek reveals new botanical wonders. Learning about these plants also enhances your appreciation of the natural environment, while connecting you to the traditional knowledge and practices of the local communities who rely on these plants for food, medicine, and cultural rituals.
### 21. Participate in a Puja Ceremony
Participating in a puja ceremony at local temples or shrines along your trek is a profound cultural experience. These traditional Hindu and Buddhist rituals involve offerings, chants, and prayers to invoke blessings and protection from the deities. Engaging in a puja allows you to witness the deep spiritual practices that are an integral part of life in the Himalayas, providing a moment of reflection and connection to the sacred aspects of the region.
### 22. Observe Traditional Farming Practices
Observing traditional farming practices in the Annapurna region offers insight into the sustainable and time-honored local methods. The terraced fields in the hillsides, intricate irrigation systems, and the cultivation of crops like rice, millet, and potatoes demonstrate a deep understanding of the land and its cycles. Local agriculture activities show the resilience and ingenuity of these communities and the importance of agriculture in their daily lives and cultural heritage.
### 23. Relax at Machapuchare Base Camp
Machapuchare Base Camp provides a perfect respite as you trek to Annapurna Base Camp. You will find yourself surrounded by the majestic beauty of Fishtail Mountain. We encourage you to take some time to unwind and take in the panoramic views of the surrounding peaks and valleys. The tranquil atmosphere and stunning scenery make it an ideal spot to rest, reflect on your journey, and recharge before continuing your adventure.
### 24. Meet Fellow Trekkers
Meeting fellow trekkers along the Annapurna trail enriches your journey with shared stories and camaraderie. Whether around a cozy teahouse fire or during a rest break on the trail, exchanging experiences and insights with other adventurers fosters a sense of community and mutual support. These connections often lead to lasting friendships and a deeper appreciation of the diverse motivations and backgrounds that bring people to the Himalayas.
### 25. Celebrate Your Achievement
Celebrating your achievement at the end of your trek to Annapurna Base Camp is a moment of immense pride and joy. Reaching this milestone signifies overcoming physical and mental challenges, and embracing the adventure of a lifetime. Sharing this triumph with fellow trekkers, guides, and locals enhances the sense of accomplishment, making it a memorable and rewarding conclusion to your Himalayan journey.
