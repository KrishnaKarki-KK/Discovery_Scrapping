# How to Prepare for Annapurna Circuit Trek
by [Paul Gurung](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/blog/author/Paul)
ON 21 Apr, 2025
![Himalayan Image](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/6193/how-to-prepare-for-annapurna-circuit-trek.webp)
The [Annapurna Circuit Trek](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/trips/annapurna-circuit-trek) is one of Nepal's most iconic trekking routes, offering diverse landscapes, incredible views, and a unique cultural experience. Spanning 160â€“230 km (depending on the route), this trek takes you through subtropical forests, high-altitude deserts, and the formidable Thorong La Pass (5,416 m above sea level).
Preparation is key for a successful and enjoyable trek, especially for beginner to intermediate trekkers. This guide tells you exactly how to prepare for Annapurna Circuit Trek.
## Annapurna Circuit Trek Fitness: How to Get in Shape
A well-rounded fitness plan is essential for the trek. It should involve long walking hours, steep ascents, and high-altitude hiking. The Annapurna Circuit demands endurance, strength, and flexibility, making a structured training regimen crucial for a smooth trekking experience.
If you are well-planned you can handle the physical and mental challenges more effectively, reducing the risk of injury and fatigue. You should start preparing a few weeks before departure, gradually increasing in intensity to build the required stamina and resilience. A mix of cardiovascular workouts, strength training, hiking simulations, and altitude adaptation exercises will help ensure peak fitness levels for the journey.
### 1. Cardiovascular Endurance
Cardiovascular endurance is the body's ability to sustain prolonged physical activity by efficiently delivering oxygen to the muscles. Since the Annapurna Circuit requires hours of walking daily, building strong cardiovascular fitness is crucial for maintaining stamina and reducing fatigue.
#### How to Improve Cardiovascular Endurance?
  * **Aerobic Exercises:** Running, cycling, swimming, and brisk walking help increase heart rate and lung capacity. Engage in at least 30â€“40 minutes of moderate to intense cardio, 4â€“5 times per week.


  * **Stair Climbing & Hill Training:** Mimicking the steep ascents of the trek by climbing stairs or hiking uphill strengthens leg muscles and improves endurance.
  * **Interval Training:** Alternate between high-intensity bursts and steady-state cardio (e.g., sprinting for 30 seconds followed by 1â€“2 minutes of light jogging). This enhances stamina and helps the body adapt to varying intensity levels encountered on the trail.


### 2. Strength Training
Cardiovascular exercise provides oxygen to muscles in your body. The strength training will strengthen your muscles. Strength training is a crucial component of physical preparation for the Annapurna Circuit Trek, as it enhances muscle endurance, stability, and resilience to handle long trekking days, steep ascents, and carrying a backpack.
#### How to Improve Strength for the Annapurna Circuit Trek?
**Leg Strength:** Since trekking involves prolonged walking and climbing, focus on exercises like:
  * **Squats** â€“ Builds lower body strength.
  * **Lunges** â€“ Improves balance and muscle endurance.
  * **Step-ups** â€“ Mimics hiking uphill with added resistance.
  * **Calf Raises** â€“ Strengthens the calves for stability on uneven terrain.


**Core Strength:** A strong core helps with posture and stability when carrying a backpack.
  * **Planks** â€“ Strengthens the entire core.
  * **Russian Twists** â€“ Enhances rotational stability.
  * **Leg Raises** â€“ Strengthens lower abdominal muscles.


**Upper Body Strength:** While the trek primarily demands lower body endurance, upper body strength helps in carrying a backpack.
  * **Push-ups** â€“ Builds chest, shoulder, and arm strength.
  * **Pull-ups** â€“ Improves back and shoulder muscles.
  * **Shoulder Presses** â€“ Strengthens shoulders for better weight distribution.


### 3. Endurance-Based Strength Training:
  * Perform exercises in higher repetitions (12-15 reps per set) with moderate weights to build muscular endurance.
  * Include bodyweight exercises and functional training to simulate trekking conditions.


### 4. Hiking Practice
Before trekking to Annapurna Circuit, You can go on long hikes with a loaded backpack to simulate trekking conditions. If you can incorporate elevation gain on these hikes, that would be awesome. You can also practice hiking for several consecutive days.
## Best Time to Trek Annapurna Circuit
Weather plays a huge role in your Annapurna Circuit trek experience. Here is a breakdown of the trekking environment in different seasons. Most trekkers find spring and autumn seasons perfect for their trekking.
  * **Spring (March-May)**


Spring brings vibrant landscapes to the Annapurna region, with rhododendrons and other wildflowers in full bloom. The weather remains mild, though lower altitudes may experience occasional rainfall. The trails are less crowded than in autumn, making it a **great alternative for those looking for a balance between good weather and fewer trekkers.** The views are stunning, the temperatures are cool, and the trails are in great shape.
  * **Summer / Monsoon (Juneâ€“August)**


Monsoon season brings**heavy rainfall,** increasing the risk of landslides and making trails slippery and difficult to navigate. The humidity attracts leeches, adding to the discomfort. Cloudy skies often obscure mountain views, reducing the scenic appeal of the trek. Due to these challenges, monsoons may not provide a great trekking experience. However, if you love water, then the cascading waterfalls and roaring rivers carry a different trekking charm.
  * **Autumn (Septemberâ€“November)**


Autumn is the**most popular season** for trekking the Annapurna Circuit, offering the best weather conditions. The skies remain clear, providing stunning mountain views, while the trails are dry and easy to navigate. Temperatures are warm in the lower regions and moderately cold at higher altitudes, making the trek comfortable. However, due to the ideal conditions, this is also the busiest time, meaning the trails and teahouses can be crowded. Major festivals, such as Tihar, Dashain, Chhat, and Indra Jatra fall during autumn making it a great season to trek.
  * **Winter (Decemberâ€“February)**


Winter trekking in the Annapurna Circuit is possible but requires extra preparation. The **temperatures can drop** significantly, especially at high altitudes like Thorong La Pass, where extreme cold can be a challenge. Snowfall may block certain trails, requiring flexibility in itinerary planning. However, for experienced trekkers who can handle the cold, winter offers solitude and fewer crowds.
## Packing List for Annapurna Circuit Trek
Packing wisely is essential for both comfort and safety on the Annapurna Circuit Trek in Nepal. The right gear will help you stay warm, dry, and energized while ensuring you are prepared for the challenges of high-altitude trekking. Since the trek spans diverse climates, from humid lowlands to freezing high passes, layering is key to adapting to changing conditions. Below is a breakdown of the must-have gear for a successful trek.
### 1. Clothing
We recommend you wear clothes in layers to regulate body temperature and stay comfortable throughout the trek. Here is what you need to pay attention to.
  * **Base Layer:** Moisture-wicking thermal tops and bottoms to keep sweat away from the skin.
  * **Insulation Layer:** A fleece or down jacket to provide warmth in colder regions.
  * **Outer Shell:** A waterproof and windproof jacket and pants to protect against rain, wind, and snow.
  * **Trekking Pants & Shirts:** Lightweight and breathable options for ease of movement.
  * **Gloves & Hat:** Insulated gloves for warmth, a beanie to retain body heat, and a sunhat for UV protection.
  * **Socks:** Wool or synthetic socks to keep feet warm and dry while preventing blisters.
  * **Gaiters:** Useful for protecting against snow, dust, and debris on the trail.


### 2. Footwear
Walking the Annapurna Circuit requires proper footwear for stability, comfort, and injury prevention on rugged trails. Here is the footwear you will need.
  * **Hiking Boots:** Bring sturdy, waterproof boots with ankle support that are well broken in, before the trek.
  * **Sandals or Camp Shoes:** Lightweight and comfortable footwear for use in teahouses or after long trekking days.


### 3. Gear & Accessories
Carrying essential trekking gear ensures convenience and safety during the journey.
  * **Backpack:** You will need two backpacks. We provide one porter for every two trekkers. Hence, partner with a fellow trekker and pack your things together in a duffle bag of over sixty liters. You will also need a regular backpack for your valuables and important documents.
  * **Sleeping Bag:** Though, tea houses/lodges provide enough bedding, bring a warm sleeping bag rated for temperatures as low as -10Â°C for added warmth.
  * **Trekking Poles:** Helps reduce impact on knees and provides better stability on uneven terrain.
  * **Headlamp & Extra Batteries:** Necessary for early morning starts and navigating in low-light conditions.
  * **Water Bottles & Purification:** Reusable bottles, along with a water filter or purification tablets to ensure safe drinking water.


### 4. Personal Items
A few additional personal items can enhance comfort and the overall trekking experience.
  * **Sunscreen & Lip Balm:** High SPF sunscreen and moisturizing lip balm to prevent sunburn and chapped lips.
  * **First Aid Kit:** Includes altitude sickness medication, band-aids, antiseptic, pain relievers, and any personal medications.
  * **Snacks & Electrolytes:** Energy bars, dried fruits, and oral rehydration salts (ORS) to maintain energy levels and hydration. We suggest you buy these in Kathmandu as it will cost you more in trek regions.
  * **Camera & Power Bank:** For capturing breathtaking views, materializing memories, and ensuring devices stay charged.


Packing strategically ensures that trekkers are well-equipped to handle the varying conditions of the Annapurna Circuit. Carrying lightweight but essential gear can make the journey smoother and more enjoyable.
## Preventing Altitude Sickness on Annapurna Circuit
The Annapurna Circuit takes trekkers through high-altitude passes, with Thorong La Pass reaching an elevation of 5,416 meters. At such heights, the risk of altitude sickness is a major concern. Common altitude sickness symptoms include headaches, dizziness, nausea, and shortness of breath. If not managed properly, it can escalate into severe conditions like High Altitude Pulmonary Edema (HAPE) or High Altitude Cerebral Edema (HACE), which can be serious.
**Proper acclimatization** and preventive measures are essential to ensure a safe and enjoyable trek. Our trek leaders carry oximeters and will use them to monitor your blood oxygen saturation levels.
One of the most effective ways to prevent altitude sickness is to schedule acclimatization days before ascending to higher altitudes. This allows the body to adjust to reduced oxygen levels gradually. Additionally, maintaining a slow and steady ascent is crucial to give the body time to adapt.
**Staying hydrated** is another key factor in preventing altitude sickness. Drinking at least 3â€“4 liters of water daily helps maintain oxygen flow and prevents dehydration, which can exacerbate symptoms. So we recommend you consume lots of fluids such as green/ginger/lemon tea and garlic soup. You should also avoid alcohol and smoking, as these substances dehydrate the body and increase the risk of altitude-related complications.
You can also use **Acetazolamide (Diamox)** as a preventive measure. This medication helps the body acclimatize more efficiently by stimulating breathing and reducing symptoms. However, it should only be taken after consulting a doctor.
Most importantly, it is crucial to **listen to your body** and recognize early signs of altitude sickness. You should inform our guide the moment you notice discomfort. If symptoms persist or worsen, our guides will take you to a lower altitude immediately. Ignoring symptoms or pushing through despite feeling unwell can be dangerous. However, you should not worry too much. In emergencies, our team will coordinate with your insurer for helicopter evacuation.
## Travel & Logistics: Permits, Accommodation, and Budget
Since you will trek with our company, we will acquire the required permits, arrange your accommodations, and provide you with full-board (main-course) meals in the trek regions. However, you will need money to cover other costs, such as souvenirs, extra meal courses, snacks, beverages (hot/cold/alcoholic), and snacks.
Hence, we recommend you allocate around USD 20 per trek day. Since the trek regions do not have money exchanges and trek regions only honor Nepali Rupees, you must exchange your currency in Kathmandu.

## Conclusion
Finally, here are the things you need for Annapurna Circuit Trek preparation.
  * **Train at least 1 month in advance** to build stamina and strength.
  * **Book flights to Kathmandu early** , especially in peak seasons and share your flight details with us for airport pick-up.
  * **Carefully read our packing list and pack things accordingly.** Remember you will need layered clothing and broken-in boots.
  * **Carry spending money in cash**(major currencies USD, EURO, GBP) and exchange it for Nepali Rupees in legal exchange currency shops in Kathmandu.
  * **Dispose of waste responsibly** and respect local culture and traditions.  
  



Preparation is important for successfully completing the Annapurna Circuit Trek. By focusing on fitness training, altitude sickness prevention, packing essentials, and timing your trek wisely, you can enhance your trekking experience and reduce risks. Whether you're a beginner or an intermediate trekker, following these tips ensures a safe, enjoyable, and unforgettable Himalayan adventure.
