# Annapurna Circuit Weather: Comprehensive Details
by [Paul Gurung](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/blog/author/Paul)
ON 21 Apr, 2025
![Himalayan Image](https://cms.discoveryworldtrekking.com/media/6262/Annapurna-Circuit-Weather-Comprehensive-Details.webp)
The [Annapurna Circuit](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/trips/annapurna-circuit-trek) is one of the most celebrated trekking routes in the world, renowned for its stunning landscapes, diverse ecosystems, and rich cultural experiences. However, understanding the weather conditions along the circuit is crucial for a successful and enjoyable trek. This guide provides comprehensive details about the Annapurna Circuit weather, including seasonal variations, temperature ranges, and the best time to trek it.
## **Overview of the Annapurna Circuit**
The Annapurna Circuit spans approximately 160-230 kilometers (depending on the route) and takes you through various climatic zones, from subtropical forests to high-altitude deserts. The trek typically starts in Besisahar and ends in Nayapul, passing through diverse landscapes, terraced fields, lush forests, and high mountain passes.
## **Understanding the Annapurna Circuit Weather**
The weather in the Annapurna region can be unpredictable, with significant variations in temperature and conditions depending on the altitude and time of year. The circuit experiences four main seasons: spring, summer, autumn, and winter. Each season offers unique weather patterns and trekking experiences.
### **1. Spring (March to May)**
Annapurna Circuit offers a perfect balance of mild temperatures and breathtaking natural beauty. As winter recedes, the trails come alive with blooming rhododendrons and lush greenery, transforming the landscape into a vibrant spectacle. The weather remains relatively stable, with clear skies providing spectacular views of the [Annapurna](https://www.discoveryworldtrekking.com/blog/annapurna-mountain-range) and Dhaulagiri ranges. While the conditions are ideal for trekking, the increasing number of visitors, particularly in April and May, means that popular routes and teahouses may become busier. Below are the key weather conditions, advantages, and considerations for trekking in spring:
**Weather Conditions:**
  * **Temperature:** Daytime temperatures range from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F) at lower altitudes, while nighttime temperatures can drop to around 0°C (32°F) at higher elevations.
  * **Precipitation:** Spring is generally dry, with occasional rain showers, especially in late May.


**Advantages:**
  * Spring is one of the best times to trek the Annapurna Circuit due to mild temperatures and blooming rhododendron flowers, creating a vibrant landscape.
  * Clear skies and excellent visibility make for stunning views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges.


**Considerations:**
  * As the season progresses, the trails can become busier with trekkers, especially in April and May.


### **2. Summer (June to August)**
Summer (June to August) coincides with the monsoon season, bringing heavy rainfall and high humidity to the Annapurna region. While the trails are less crowded, the frequent downpours can make trekking more challenging, with slippery paths and a higher risk of landslides in certain areas. 
Despite these difficulties, the landscape is at its most vibrant, with lush greenery, roaring rivers, and cascading waterfalls adding to the dramatic scenery. However, cloud cover and rain showers can obscure mountain views, reducing visibility at higher elevations. Below are the key weather conditions, advantages, and considerations for trekking in summer:
**Weather Conditions:**
  * **Temperature:** Daytime temperatures can reach 25°C (77°F) in lower regions, while higher altitudes may see temperatures around 10°C (50°F) during the day and dip to 0°C
  * **Precipitation:** This season marks the monsoon period, with heavy rainfall, particularly in July and August.


**Advantages:**
  * The landscape is lush and green, with waterfalls and rivers flowing abundantly.
  * Fewer trekkers on the trail can provide a more peaceful experience.


**Considerations:**
  * The heavy rainfall can lead to landslides and trail closures, making trekking challenging and potentially dangerous.
  * Visibility may be poor due to clouds and rain, limiting views of the mountains.


### **3. Autumn (September to November)**
Autumn (September to November) is an ideal season for trekking the Annapurna Circuit, offering stable weather, clear skies, and breathtaking mountain views. As the monsoon season recedes in September, the trails dry up, revealing crisp, panoramic vistas of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges. The moderate temperatures during the day make for comfortable trekking conditions, while the cool, fresh air enhances the overall experience. 
While the trails are generally less crowded than in spring, October is the peak trekking month, attracting many adventurers. As the season progresses into November, temperatures at higher altitudes can drop significantly, requiring proper gear for cold conditions. Below are the key weather conditions, advantages, and considerations for trekking in autumn:
**Weather Conditions:**
  * **Temperature:** Daytime temperatures range from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F) at lower altitudes, while nighttime temperatures can drop to around -5°C (41°F) at higher elevations.
  * **Precipitation:** The monsoon season ends in September, leading to clearer skies and drier conditions.


**Advantages:**
  * Autumn is the best time to trek the Annapurna Circuit, with stable weather, clear skies, and stunning mountain views.
  * The trails are less crowded than in spring, especially in late October and November.


**Considerations:**
  * As the season progresses, temperatures can drop significantly, especially at higher altitudes, so trekkers should be prepared for colder conditions.  
  



### **4. Winter (December to February)**
Winter (December to February) transforms the Annapurna Circuit into a serene and snow-covered wonderland, offering a unique trekking experience with fewer crowds. The cold temperatures and potential snowfall at higher elevations present a significant challenge, making this season suitable for well-prepared and experienced trekkers. 
While the lower altitudes remain manageable with daytime temperatures above freezing, the nights can be bitterly cold, especially in the higher mountain passes. The reward for braving the winter conditions is unparalleled tranquility, as well as stunning views of snow-cloaked peaks under crisp, clear skies. However, some high-altitude teahouses may close for the season, requiring careful planning for accommodations. Below are the key weather conditions, advantages, and considerations for trekking in winter:
**Weather Conditions:**
  * **Temperature:** Daytime temperatures can range from 5°C to 15°C (41°F to 59°F) at lower altitudes, while nighttime temperatures can plummet to -10°C (14°F) or lower at higher elevations.
  * **Precipitation:** Winter is generally dry, but snowfall can occur, especially at higher altitudes.


**Advantages:**
  * The Annapurna region is less crowded, providing a more solitary trekking experience.
  * The snow-covered landscapes offer a unique and beautiful perspective of the mountains.


**Considerations:**
  * Cold temperatures and potential snowfall can make trekking challenging, especially at higher elevations.
  * Some teahouses may be closed during the winter months, limiting accommodation options.


## **Best Time to trek Annapurna Circuit**
The best time to trek the Annapurna Circuit is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) seasons. These periods offer the most favorable weather conditions, with mild temperatures, clear skies, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
### **Spring Trekking**
  * **March to April:** Ideal for those who enjoy blooming flora and moderate temperatures. The trails are less crowded than in May, making it a great time for a peaceful trek.
  * **May:** The weather starts to warm up, but it can also be busier as many trekkers take advantage of the favorable conditions before the monsoon.


### **Autumn Trekking**
  * **September:** The weather is still settling after the monsoon, with occasional rain but improving conditions. It's a good time for those who prefer fewer crowds and are willing to deal with some residual moisture.
  * **October:** This month is often considered the peak trekking season, with stable weather, clear skies, and vibrant autumn colors. The trails are busier, but the experience is rewarding with breathtaking views.
  * **November:** As temperatures begin to drop, the crowds thin out, making it a great time for those seeking solitude while still enjoying good weather.


## **Preparing for Weather Variations**
Regardless of the season, trekkers should be prepared for sudden weather changes. Here are some tips for dealing with the Annapurna Circuit weather:
  * **Layered Clothing:** Dress in layers to adjust to varying temperatures throughout the day. Start with moisture-wicking base layers, add insulating layers, and finish with a waterproof outer layer.
  * **Weather-Appropriate Gear:** Invest in good quality trekking gear, including a reliable rain jacket, warm gloves, and a hat. Proper footwear is essential for navigating diverse terrains.
  * **Hydration and Nutrition:** Stay hydrated and maintain energy levels with nutritious snacks, as altitude can affect appetite and hydration needs.
  * **Check Weather Forecasts:** Regularly check local weather forecasts and be prepared to adjust your itinerary if conditions change unexpectedly.


## **Conclusion**
Understanding the Annapurna Circuit weather is essential for planning a successful trek. By choosing the right season and preparing for the conditions, trekkers can fully enjoy the stunning landscapes and rich cultural experiences that this iconic route has to offer. Whether you trek in the vibrant spring or the crisp autumn, the Annapurna Circuit promises an unforgettable adventure in the heart of the Himalayas.
